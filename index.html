José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda June 19, 1861, Calamba December 30, 1896, Rizal Park, Manila He wrote the novels Noli Me Tángere (1887) and El filibusterismo (1891), which together are taken as a national epic, in addition to numerous poems and essays.
Apolinario Mabini y Maranan July 23, 1864, Talaga, Tanauan May 13, 1903, Manila a Filipino revolutionary leader, educator, lawyer, and statesman who served first as a legal and constitutional adviser to the Revolutionary Government, and then as the first Prime Minister of the Philippines upon the establishment of the First Philippine Republic.
Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro November 30, 1863, Tondo May 10, 1897, Maragondon a Filipino revolutionary leader. He is often called "The Father of the Philippine Revolution", and considered a national hero of the Philippines.
Marcelo Hilario del Pilar y Gatmaitán August 30, 1850, Bulacan July 4, 1896, Old Hospital de la Santa Creu, Barcelona, Spain a Filipino writer, lawyer, journalist, and freemason. Del Pilar, along with José Rizal and Graciano López Jaena, became known as the leaders of the Reform Movement in Spain.
Antonio Narciso Luna de San Pedro y Novicio Ancheta October 29, 1866, Binondo, Manila June 5, 1899, Cabanatuan City a Filipino army general and a pharmacist who fought in the Philippine–American War before his assassination on June 5, 1899, at the age of 32.
